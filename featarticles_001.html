FROM IDEAS TO FRAMES

Written by Emmanuel Patrick Published on June 15, 2025 5 min read

I've always had a great love for storytelling. I believe I'm naturally creative and talented—a generational talent, if I dare say. However, I'm slowly beginning to understand that just because you're the first to stand doesn't mean you'll be the first to walk.

I've met incredibly talented people in my life—people whose gifts bruise my ego every single day. It's a feeling I have a love-hate relationship with.

Realizing I'm not as amazing as I once thought means I get to do so much more, become so much more—and that gives me joy. It comforts me to know I haven't yet reached my greatest self, because it would be quite depressing to say this is my final form. But that's not entirely a pleasant feeling either. It's like climbing a very tall ladder, believing that just beyond the clouds lies the end—only to realize the ladder keeps going, and you can't see where it stops. You just have faith that it will stop, and that when it does, you'll feel fulfilled.

"Let's take a moment to look at this ladder. What if it never stops, and you just keep climbing until your body gives out? What if the pursuit is endless—and you're only now realizing the possibility of that?"

You've already committed to the climb, too far up to go back down. What would you say to this person now questioning their choices? Are you this person?

I won't claim to have the answer to this predicament, but I will say that I've chosen to love the process far more than whatever might be waiting for me at the end. Each step feels rewarding and fulfilling in its own way.

I still believe I'm talented and hardworking—and I pat myself on the back for the good I've done. I'm good, but not the best. And when it comes to art, it feels like everyone is the best to someone. So, focus on being your best.

The entire concept of making a film once felt otherworldly to me. I used to stare at huge blockbuster movies as a child, eyes wide with wonder. Even now, I still feel the same magic behind them. My taste in film has evolved over the years; my palette has become, what I believe, refined. But at the core, my appreciation for film—and for all other forms of art—remains the same, if not more elevated.
